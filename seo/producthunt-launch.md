## First Comment — Maker Story (500 words)

Hey Product Hunt — I'm Kyle, founder of MyClawAgent.

I want to tell you what actually led to building this, because it wasn't a lightbulb moment. It was a slow burn of frustration.

About eight months ago I started experimenting with OpenClaw — an open-source AI agent platform that goes way beyond what ChatGPT does. We're talking a real agent: connected to your Gmail, Google Calendar, Telegram, your CRM. Something that actually executes tasks autonomously instead of just answering questions. I was hooked immediately. The potential was obvious.

The setup was not obvious.

I spent the better part of two weekends getting it configured properly. OAuth flows that kept breaking. API keys that needed exact formatting. Security hardening that most tutorials either skip or get wrong. A system prompt that took hours of iteration before the agent started behaving like I actually wanted it to. I'm technical enough to push through all of that. But I kept thinking: there is no world where the busy founders I know are going to do this.

And that was the problem. The people who would benefit most from a capable AI agent — founders running multiple things, CEOs drowning in operational noise, exec teams spending hours on stuff that should be automated — are exactly the people who don't have time to configure Node.js environments on a Sunday afternoon.

So I started setting it up for other people.

First it was a couple of colleagues who'd heard me talk about it. Then word spread. What I found every time was the same: once the agent was properly deployed and configured, people didn't want to go back. Not because it was magic, but because having something reliable handling email triage, calendar management, and CRM updates at all hours is genuinely useful in a way that's hard to describe until you've experienced it.

MyClawAgent is the productized version of that service.

Here's how it works: you book a call, we scope your setup, and we deploy your OpenClaw agent to a private secured environment the same day. We connect it to your tools, configure the skills you actually need, and walk you through how to delegate to it. Then we manage it ongoing — updates, fixes, new capabilities as you need them — for a flat monthly retainer.

What makes it different from just "AI setup services":

We're OpenClaw specialists. We know the platform deeply, which means faster setup and better security hygiene. We don't hand you a config file and disappear. Ongoing management means your agent stays functional as tools and APIs change. And because everything runs in your own environment, your data stays yours.

This is $3,000 for onboarding and $199.99/month ongoing. Not cheap, not enterprise — it's priced for the founder who values their time at more than minimum wage.

If you've been curious about AI agents but haven't found a setup path that actually works without a technical co-founder: this is for you.

Happy to answer any questions below. If you've tried setting up OpenClaw yourself and hit walls, I especially want to hear from you — those conversations usually lead to better product decisions.

— Kyle, MyClawAgent
